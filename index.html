About Me

I am first year PhD student at Stanford University in the Computer Science Department. My research interests broadly lie in the advancement of AI-driven decision-making, and I actively work in the subjects of reinforcement learning, experimental design and causal inference.

I previously graduated from MIT with a MEng in Computer Science and Electrical Engineering in 2025 and a Bachelor's in Mathematics and in Artificial Intelligence in 2024. During this time, I had the pleasure of working with Professors Caroline Uhler and Aldo Pacchiano on the topics of causal representation learning and experimental design as an Eric and Wendy Schmidt Center Scholar at the Broad Institute.
